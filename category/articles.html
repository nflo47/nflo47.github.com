Summer Reading

 June 16 2013

  1. Python Programming: An Introduction to Computer Science by John Zelle Most often, going through python lessons is a drag in which one feels totally lost. But, it's always rewarding when a finished product is involved (e.g., this blog), and I hope to generate more tangible goods soon. Looking to do something more "data analysis" related next time to pair with my science background.

  2. The Man In the High Castle by Philip k. Dick A romp through an alternate history years after WWII where the Germans won and an arms race is occurring between them and the Japanese. It's a tale of an alternative present, questioning our perception of reality and the records that follow.

  3. Ted Chiang's short story collection, Story of Your Life and Others One of my favorites from the book was originally published in Nature as Catching Crumbs from the Table. It is a futuristic look at a research world dominated by metahumans, causing a knowledge gap for human scientists. Not as dystopian as it sounds.

  4. Many articles and a few books on current trends in biotech and healthcare. On deck is a book touting the benefits of personalized healthcare, titled The Creative Destruction of Medicine.

  5. In Search of Lost Time, vol 1: Swann's Way by Marcel Proust The passages in this book will exercise your memory facilities, conjuring up thoughts, feelings, you have not revisited in many years. 2013 also marks the 100th anniversary of its publication.
